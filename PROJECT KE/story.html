What's project 21?

Project 21KE is a Youth initiative group founded by 5 UWC-Amala alumni from different nationalities,in Kakuma refugee camp on the 28th of February 2021. These students have commonalities in that they are passionate about giving back to their communities and transforming the livelihood of the vulnerable members of the society,women, in Kakuma refugee camp. The organisation was formed with the desire of making change in their community and later will extend it to the world. The organisationâ€™s main objective is to address needs-based problems mainly in the Kakuma refugee camp. The organisation believes in transforming the livelihood of the vulnerable members of society,women and creating a more sustainable future.

Where is Kakuma Refugee Camp?

Kakuma Refugee camp is a UNHCR run camp located in the desert of Northwestern Kenya. It was founded in 1992 after the arrival of the lost Boys of Sudan who were uprooted during Sudan's Civil War. The camp houses over 200,000 people from many of the surrounding countries including South Sudan, Somalia, Ethiopia, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.Life in the camp is far from easy; on top of the harsh conditions of the desert and a temperature that can reach 104 degrees fahrenheit during the day, the camp also has a high poverty rate and food insecurity is a common threat. In Kakuma refugees are almost completely dependent on international humanitarian assistance for food and other supplies.

WHAT WE DO

We design and initiate sustainable programs and projects aimed at solving challenges that affect the most vulnerable members of our society,women and children in Kakuma Refugee Camp and give them additional continuous support. We assess how we can help them meet their needs and how to extend their services to other members of the camp. Then we use our network to provide them with support tailored to their needs.

The support we offer comes in two forms: monetary support,training,follow up,management and volunteer support.

WHY WE DO WHAT WE DO

We believe that the vulnerable members of our society,women are strong and can change the world and contribute to productivity when given opportunities and support they need to thrive! We work to find the most vulnerable members of our society, women with potential and the will to create change in their lives and provide them with proper support to help them become skillful, productive,self-reliant,improve their living standards and that of the community at large.

HOW WE DO IT

We team up with individuals,organisations,well wishers and donors to empower our beneficiaries through our various sustainable programs and projects aimed at solving challenges that affect their living standards.

Our Vision

To eradicate extreme poverty, food insecurity and create self-reliance among the most vulnerable members of our society,women,in Kakuma refugee camp.

Our Mission

Design and initiate sustainable programs and projects aimed at solving challenges that affect the most vulnerable members of our society,women.
